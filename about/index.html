|- - - about this blog - - -|

So I've been reading a lot of online blogs throught the years (Crackberry, Hackaday), and I've always wanted to have somewhere to share my thoughts and my projects.

Some of you might know I've got a youtube channel, but honestly I have a lot of trouble with finishing a video, let alone a decent one. So I'd like to focus on something I feel I can keep up with. Hopefully I can update here somewhat consistently, and pardon any spelling mistakes since I'm using a code editor rather than a word processor.

If you're interested in the source code of this blog it is available here. It is designed to be compatible with old browsers, specifically the BlackBerry 8520's. I do know that Jekyll has support for blogs, but doing it this way makes it easier for me to do custom cool stuff.

|- - - about me - - -|

I'm Demma, mexican trans girl, higly interested in older tech (as you can probably tell).

Since I was young I've liked to tinker with stuff , learning about how everything works. From disasembling computers to fixing a Gameboy Color , my hobbies sometimes involve bringing new life to older devices. I'm fascinated to how versatile older tech was, how well thought out it was.

If you look into my backpack you'll find a PSP with a 128gb SD card , a purple Gameboy Color with an Everdrive , a pair of pliers , string and needle and a bunch of other "useless" things that I carry arround for when they are needed.

I've been doing small stuff ever since I learned how to program Batch from the Notepad on windows , some of that stuff has come out to be not so small in fact. I'm the proud developer of 3s-psp, a Street Fighter 3rd Strike port for the PSP , along with giblet from the 3sx discord server.

I've always wanted to make life easier with simple software , and to make a living from it. Still haven't figured out that 2nd part , but the 1st one is what I want to focus on. I want to make software responsive again , using minimal resources and being catered to 1 issue , not trying to be the "everything" app.
